\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Linear combination:} Forms like \(3\bm{v}+5\bm{w}\), \(\bm{v}\) and \(\bm{w}\) are vectors.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Why equations fail}

For example:
\(
  \begin{array}{l}
    c+2d = 1 \\
    c+3d=0 \\
    c+4d=0
  \end{array}
\) fail?
Because it's actually a linear combination \(
  \begin{bmatrix}
    1 \\ 1 \\ 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  +d
  \begin{bmatrix}
    2 \\ 3 \\ 4
  \end{bmatrix}
\)
and the solve is the intersect vector, if no vector can satisfies the conditions then there is no solve.
